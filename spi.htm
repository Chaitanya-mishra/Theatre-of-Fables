ॐ नमः शिवाय।।

Vidyapati, a devoted poet of Lord Shiva, was visited by Shiva himself disguised as a servant named Ugna. Shiva served Vidyapati humbly, asking only for food, and promised not to reveal his true identity. One day, when Vidyapati was extremely thirsty, Ugna brought him water from the Ganga flowing from his hair, revealing his divine nature. Vidyapati promised to keep the secret. Later, when Vidyapati's wife angrily tried to beat Ugna, Vidyapati revealed Ugna’s identity to protect him. Immediately, Lord Shiva disappeared, leaving Vidyapati heartbroken but blessed. Shiva promised to remain with him in the form of a Shivling for worship.

This story highlights profound devotion, humility, and the close bond between a devotee and the divine.


ॐ नमो भगवते वासुदेवाय।।

Lord Vishnu took the form of Varaha, a mighty boar, to rescue the Earth goddess Bhumi Devi when the demon Hiranyaksha abducted her and hid her deep in the cosmic ocean. Vishnu as Varaha dove into the ocean, battled and killed the demon, and lifted Bhumi Devi on his tusks, restoring her to her rightful place in the universe. This avatar symbolizes the restoration of cosmic order and protection of the Earth from evil forces.


ॐ श्री गणेशाय नमः।।

Lord Ganesha was created by Goddess Parvati when she made a boy out of clay or turmeric paste and breathed life into him to guard her while she bathed. Obedient, Ganesha stopped Lord Shiva from entering. Not knowing Ganesha was his son, Shiva cut off the boy’s head in anger. To console Parvati, Shiva replaced Ganesha’s head with that of a powerful elephant and brought him back to life. Shiva then declared Ganesha to be the leader of his attendants and the remover of obstacles, blessing him to be worshipped first before any endeavor.


या देवी सर्वभूतेषु शक्तिरूपेण संस्थिता, नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमस्तस्यै नमो नमः।।

Once Mahishasura, a demon who could take many forms, terrorized the heavens and the earth, defeating all gods. To save the world, Goddess Durga, a fierce and powerful warrior goddess, fought him for nine days. Mahishasura changed shapes to escape her strikes, but Durga’s strength and divine weapons overcame each form. On the tenth day, she defeated him by beheading the buffalo demon with her trident, restoring peace and balance. This victory is celebrated as a symbol of good triumphing over evil during the festival of Durga Puja.


ॐ श्री कृष्णः शरणं ममः।।

One famous story of Lord Krishna from his childhood is when he defeated the serpent Kaliya. Kaliya, a poisonous, multi-headed serpent, was poisoning the Yamuna River where Krishna and his friends played. To save the villagers and the river, young Krishna fearlessly jumped into the water, danced on Kaliya’s many heads, and subdued him. Kaliya promised to leave the Yamuna and never return, restoring the river's purity. This story shows Krishna's courage and divine power to protect and bring peace.
